About Me

With over 15 years of experience in marine biology and aquatic ecosystem research, I specialize in water quality analysis, environmental impact assessment, and marine conservation strategies.

My passion for aquatic sciences began during my undergraduate studies at the Scripps Institution of Oceanography, where I first experienced the complexity and beauty of marine ecosystems. Since then, I have dedicated my career to understanding the intricate relationships between aquatic organisms and their environments.

I have conducted extensive fieldwork across diverse aquatic environments, from coral reefs in the Pacific to freshwater lakes in the Great Lakes region. My research focuses on the impact of climate change on aquatic ecosystems, sustainable fisheries management, and innovative water treatment technologies.
